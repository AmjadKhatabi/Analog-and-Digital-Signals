Built-in Periodic Signal Functions

The gensig command is used in for generating periodic sines, square pulses, and unit pulses. Its format is as:
[s , t0] = gensig(‘type’, T, t, ts)
where:
type: is the signal type, such as ‘sin’ ‘square’, ‘sawtooth’ or ‘pulse’
T: is the period of the signal
t: is the signal duration ts is the sampling time, the default value is 1 ms
s: the dependent variable
t0: the ndependent variable

Example

Use the gensig command to generate a square pulse of a period of three seconds, for a time duration of 20 seconds, set the sampling time to 0.001 seconds.
[amp , tm ] = gensig('square', 3.5, 35 , 0.001) ;
plot(tm,amp) ;
axis([0 35 -0.2 1.2])
grid on
title('Gensig command');

Time Shifting

Consider a signal g(t) and the same signal delayed by T seconds which we shall denote by φ(t). Whatever happens in g(t) at some instant t also happens in φ(t) T seconds later at the instant t + T. Therefore
Shift is to the right (delay), means that whatever happens at time t in g(t) it will happen T later in φ(t). φ(t) is a delayed representation of g(t)
Shift is to the left (advancement), means whatever happens in time t in φ(t), it will happen in T later in g(t) φ(t) is an advanced representation of g(t)
